How it works

Three steps to never typing the same thing twice

Unfurl watches your keyboard silently in the background. The moment it recognises a trigger, it instantly replaces it — in any app, any window.

1
Create a shortcut

Give it a short trigger word and the full text you want it to expand into. Organise into categories if you like.

2
Type your trigger

Type the trigger anywhere — emails, chat apps, code editors, browsers. Unfurl sees it the moment you finish.

3
Watch it expand

In a flash the trigger is replaced with your full text, perfectly formatted, exactly where your cursor is.

Features

Everything you need, nothing you don't

Unfurl is fast, focused, and built to stay out of your way.

Works everywhere

Expands in any Windows application — browsers, email clients, Slack, Word, terminals, you name it.

Dynamic variables

Insert today's date, time, day, month or year automatically into any expansion.

Categories & search

Organise shortcuts into categories and filter or search instantly. Drag to reorder.

App blocking

Disable expansion in specific apps like password managers or terminals where it shouldn't fire.

Favourites & tray

Pin your most-used shortcuts as favourites and copy them instantly from the system tray.

Import & export

Back up your shortcuts to JSON and restore them anywhere. Share sets with teammates.

Trigger prefix support

Set an optional prefix character (like ;) so triggers only fire intentionally — type ;thanks not just thanks. Eliminates accidental expansions completely.

Dynamic variables

Live values, automatically

Drop any of these into your replacement text and Unfurl will substitute them with real values at the moment of expansion — no copy-pasting dates ever again.

{date}23/04/2026
{time}09:30 AM
{datetime}full stamp
{day}Thursday
{month}April
{year}2026
